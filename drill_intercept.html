Notes methodology + drill-stage thresholds

1. AuEq formula. NI 43-101 / JORC standard: AuEq = Σ(grade × price × recovery) / (Au_price × Au_recovery). Each metal row carries its own price and recovery — edit them to match your assumptions. Defaults: Au $2,400/oz @ 90%, Ag $28/oz @ 75%, Cu $4.20/lb @ 88%, Pb $0.95/lb @ 80%, Zn $1.30/lb @ 85%, Ni $8/lb @ 70%, Mo $20/lb @ 80%, Co $15/lb @ 65%, Sn $14/lb @ 72%, Li₂O $14.50/lb @ 75%, U₃O₈ $80/lb @ 90%.

2. Multi-intercept holes. A drill hole can have several mineralised zones at different depths. Add as many intercepts as your assays show — each gets its own AuEq, true width, and gram-meters. The hole's "Total g·m" is the sum across intercepts; "Best g·m" is the largest single intercept. Save the whole hole as one record; the saved-holes table shows hole-level rollups.

3. Gram-meters and ranking. The universal cross-commodity ranking metric: g·m = AuEq grade × true width. Rank tile compares your active intercept against the 46-hole benchmark set. The benchmark scatter's iso-curves let you see the grade-vs-width trade-off explicitly.

4. Project scatter — drilling universe + filters. Same log-log axes as the benchmark chart (true width × AuEq grade). Background cloud is 4,188 real drill intercepts across 1,180 projects, coloured by commodity group (Au / Cu / Li / Ni / U / Zn / Other). True width is assumed equal to downhole length — the source spreadsheet has no TW indicator, so all g·m figures are upper bounds. Filters: click a commodity pill to toggle that group on/off (also hides its KDE envelope and removes its projects from the summary table); drag the Min g·m slider to suppress low-significance intercepts; type into Project search for autocomplete from the universe. Matched-project halos always show, regardless of filter state. Toggle KDE contours to overlay per-commodity envelopes (50% solid fill = inner half of mass; 80% dashed = outer 80%). Envelopes are computed live in-browser as a Gaussian fit on log10(width) × log10(AuEq grade) — every filter change (commodity, min g·m, scope-to-table, project search) reshapes the contour. Initial scipy KDE on the full universe lives in drill_universe.json as a baseline reference but is no longer rendered. Stage indicator below tracks # holes > 100 g·m for your saved holes. The summary table below the chart ranks all universe projects on n intercepts / best g·m / median g·m / # >100 g·m / # >1k g·m — click a row to highlight that project, or toggle Scope chart to table to filter the chart's cloud + KDE to just the visible top-50 rows.

Holes need to be spatially correlated to count as a deposit — random scattered hits don't aggregate. Narrow-vein bonanza deposits (Fosterville, Brucejack) need many more holes per ton mineable than bulk porphyries (Detour, Hemi).

5. Commodity coloring. Benchmark dots are coloured by the metal that contributes the most to AuEq at v1 default prices: Au, Cu, U₃O₈, Li₂O, or Polymetallic (no single metal > 70% of AuEq). Your active intercept is a green diamond with a soft halo.

6. Saved data. Stored in your browser's localStorage under key zovlyn_drill_holes (records from the v1 schema auto-migrate on load). Private to this browser; not synced. Clearing browser data wipes the list.

7. Out of scope (still v3). No raw-assay compositor (still summary-style intercepts), no NSR / smelter terms, no true-width-from-angles calc, no spatial / coordinate handling. Deposit drilling profiles are approximate — verify in source NI 43-101 reports before quoting. Not investment advice.
