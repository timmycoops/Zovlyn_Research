Notes methodology

1. Tonnage refers to total in-situ resource (or any subset — measured + indicated, inferred, or P&P reserves). Inputs to this tool are not stage-specific.

2. Li2O grade should be reported to 2 decimals from the company's MRE or NI 43-101 Technical Report. Convert from Li (ppm) using ÷ 0.4644 if needed.

3. Recovery is plant-process recovery applied to in-situ Li2O units. Default 75% reflects current generation DMS circuits at industry standard. Flotation typically 65–72%; bulk-sorted DMS can reach 80–85%.

4. Concentrate (SC6) is back-calculated assuming a 6.0% Li2O spec. Real concentrate grades range 5.5–6.5%; adjust accordingly.

5. Stoichiometric LCE is the theoretical maximum, used for EV/t LCE peer comparison; recovered LCE is what would actually report to a carbonate plant.
