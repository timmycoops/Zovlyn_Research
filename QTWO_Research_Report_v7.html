[01] PROJECT OVERVIEW 801 claims · 41,253 ha · QC

The Cisco Lithium Project is located in the Eeyou Istchee James Bay region of Quebec, Canada — the same geological corridor hosting Patriot Battery Metals' Shaakichiuwaanaan and Nemaska's Whabouchi. The project comprises 801 mineral claims totaling 41,253 hectares, with the main mineralized zone just 6.5 km from the Billy Diamond Highway.

Q2 Metals announced the inaugural NI 43-101 Inferred Mineral Resource Estimate on April 20, 2026, establishing Cisco as one of the largest undeveloped hard-rock lithium deposits globally.

▸ Geology

Single continuous spodumene pegmatite body. Strike length: 1.8 km. True thickness ranges from 2 m to over 450 m. Near-surface with potential for large-scale open pit. Hosted in Archaean metasediments of the La Grande geological sub-province.

[04] PEER VALUATION · EV / t LCE

At ~$7/t LCE on an EV basis, QTWO trades at a significant discount to peers. PMET peaked at ~$50/t LCE pre-resource. Post-MRE, the re-rate thesis hinges on the market pricing Cisco's 9.1 Mt contained LCE closer to peer multiples as the project de-risks through PEA and eventually FS.

[04.1] COST CURVE · WHERE WOULD CISCO SIT?

Benchmarking against Quebec peers, Cisco would likely sit in the $650–800/t SC6 range on the hard-rock cost curve — comfortably below the marginal producer but above Tier-1 Australian operations. Scale (295 Mt, 20-year mine life) supports lower unit costs than smaller James Bay projects.
