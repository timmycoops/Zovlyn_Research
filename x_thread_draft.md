### Post 2 / The Demand Side
The demand picture is clearer than people think — you just need to look at GWh deployed, not price cycles.

Battery deployments hit 1,300 GWh in 2024. At 25% CAGR (IEA baseline), that's 4,000+ GWh by 2030.

BESS alone is compounding at 35%+. Commercial EV/trucks at 30%. This isn't speculative — these are orders being placed today.

### Post 3 / The Supply Problem
Now flip to supply. When you probability-weight the pipeline (DFS projects at 50%, PFS at 35%, exploration at 15%), the "announced supply" drops by 20-30%.

Half the projects in feasibility studies won't make it on time. Permitting delays, financing gaps, community opposition.

Risk-adjusted supply barely keeps pace with the LOW demand scenario.

### Post 4 / The Cost Curve Tells You Who Survives
At current spod spot (~$2,300/t SC6), basically the whole hard rock cost curve is green. That's why miners are restarting.

But drag that slider back to $1,050 and watch how fast it flips. Greenbushes at $220/t survives anything. Half the industry doesn't survive $800.

The marginal producer sets the floor. The supply gap sets the ceiling.
