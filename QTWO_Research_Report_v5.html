The Lithium Supply Gap

Battery deployments hit 1,300 GWh in 2024 and are compounding at 25%+ annually. BESS alone is growing at 35% CAGR. When you probability-weight the development pipeline (DFS at 50%, PFS at 35%, exploration at 15%), announced supply drops 20-30%. The structural deficit emerges by 2028-2029 under base case assumptions and widens dramatically through the 2030s.

Hard Rock Cost Curve — Who Survives?

At current SC6 spot (~$2,300/t), the entire hard rock cost curve is profitable. Greenbushes at $220/t is in a class of its own. The Australian mid-tier clusters around $500-650/t. Development-stage projects in Quebec estimate $700-870/t. The marginal producer sets the floor price — when spod drops below their cost, they shut down and supply tightens.

Location & Infrastructure

The Cisco Lithium Project is located in the Eeyou Istchee James Bay region of Quebec — the same geological corridor as Patriot's Shaakichiuwaanaan and Nemaska's Whabouchi. The project comprises 801 mineral claims totaling 41,253 hectares.

Geology & Drill Results

The Cisco deposit is a continuous spodumene pegmatite body extending 1.8 km in strike length with thickness ranging from 2m to over 450m. The MRE establishes 295Mt of inferred mineral resources at 1.36% Li2O — making it one of the largest undeveloped hard rock lithium deposits globally. 74 drill holes for 31,961m have been completed to date.
