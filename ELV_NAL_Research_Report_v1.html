[01] ASSET & OPERATOR NAL · La Corne · Québec

The North American Lithium ("NAL") operation is a hard-rock spodumene mine and concentrator in La Corne, in the Abitibi-Témiscamingue region of Québec — ~550 km north of Montréal, 60 km north of Val-d'Or, 38 km southeast of Amos. The plant restarted in March 2023 and is currently permitted for 4,500 tpd of average annual milling. Elevra Lithium (ASX:ELV, NASDAQ:ELVR), the renamed entity following the Sayona Mining / Piedmont Lithium merger, is the operator.

Current run-rate concentrate production is roughly 200 ktpa SC5.4. The Updated Scoping Study released 12 May 2026 lays out a three-stage debottlenecking pathway to 338 ktpa, sequenced inside the existing permit envelope before stepping out to 6,500 tpd. The study is preliminary — it is a scoping study, not a PFS — and is underpinned solely by current Ore Reserves rather than Inferred resource.

[02] UPDATED SCOPING STUDY · 12 MAY 2026 Vs. base 15 Sep 2025

The update reframes the September 2025 whole-of-project expansion as a staged debottlenecking. Total initial CAPEX is unchanged at US$270M (C$366M), but spend is now split across three discrete tranches; first incremental tonnes land mid-CY27 — two years earlier than originally contemplated. Average LOM unit cash cost is essentially unchanged (US$628/t C1 vs. prior); the post-tax NPV uplift is split roughly evenly between the staging benefit and a higher Li2O price deck (Benchmark Mineral Intelligence Q1 2026).

[03] MINE PLAN, RESERVES & RESOURCES As at 30 Jun 2025 · JORC 2012

The expansion is underpinned solely by current Ore Reserves — no Inferred component in the production target. NAL Reserves grew 124% to 48.6 Mt @ 1.11% Li2O in the August 2025 update. Open-pit mining over 21 years (vs. 35 years at the base / unexpanded rate), maximum pit depth ~380 m below surface, seven mining phases. Past underground workings constrained phase walls; current LOM plan is clear of historical workings by end-2030.

[05] PEER POSITIONING · COST CURVE

Post-expansion C1 of US$628/t places NAL meaningfully below the marginal producer and inside the second cost quartile of named global hard-rock producers and near-term restarts — well above Greenbushes and Pilgangoora, but ahead of Australian peers like Mt Marion, Bald Hill and several Quebec / Ontario projects. Stage 3 is what gets NAL there; before Stage 3, NAL sits closer to the bottom of the second quartile.

[06] CATALYSTS
[✓]
Updated Scoping Study released 12 MAY 2026 · COMPLETE

Staged pathway crystallised. Total CAPEX unchanged at US$270M, NPV uplift 102% to C$969M incremental / C$3,112M total post-tax. Two-year acceleration of first incremental tonnes vs. Sep 2025 study.

[→]
Stage 2 permit (6,500 tpd) CRITICAL PATH

The current mill permit is set at 4,500 tpd. Stages 2 and 3 require permit amendment for 6,500 tpd of average annual milling. This is explicitly called out as the critical path constraint.

[→]
Funding decisions 2026–2027

Total initial CAPEX C$366M (US$270M) sequenced across three stages — Stage 1 funding the smallest commitment (C$96M). Study language flags "no certainty" of funding when needed, and warns of potential dilution. Watch for cash position, debt facility / strategic prepayment news flow.

[→]
PFS-level study EXPECTED

The May 2026 release remains a Scoping Study — preliminary, not investment grade. A PFS upgrade is the next de-risking step and would normally precede the Stage 2 capital commitment.

[●]
Stage 1 commissioning MID-CY27

Within current permit envelope. Delivers a 15–20% lift over current ~200 ktpa run rate. Should also begin to bend the unit-cost curve down.

[~]
SC6 price EXOGENOUS

Study price deck is Benchmark Q1 2026 — LOM SC6 varies US$1,260–2,430/t. Long-term US$2,430 from 2036. ~49% of the NPV uplift in the May update came from the higher price deck vs. Sep 2025. Price remains the dominant swing factor.

[~]
Underground Inferred resource UPSIDE

10.3 Mt @ 1.01% Li2O Inferred underground sits outside the current production target. Conversion to a defined Reserve via further drilling could extend mine life beyond the current 21 years.

[06.1] KEY RISKS
STUDY LEVELScoping-grade only. Company's own cautionary statement: "insufficient to provide assurance of an economic development case at this stage". Numbers will move at PFS.
PERMITTINGStage 2 / 3 require amendment to lift the average annual milling permit from 4,500 to 6,500 tpd. Stage 1 stays inside the existing envelope; Stages 2 and 3 do not. Identified as the critical path.
PRICE~49% of the NPV uplift vs. the prior study came from a higher Li2O price deck. LOM-average study price US$2,154/t SC6 against recent spot in the US$700–900/t band would compress economics materially.
FUNDINGStudy explicitly notes no certainty of funding and risk of equity dilution. C$96M Stage 1 is manageable; cumulative C$366M through Stage 3 is not trivial relative to current cash.
EXECUTIONStage 2 routes feed through a temporary mobile crushing circuit operating alongside the existing crusher before the Stage 3 permanent replacement. Operational risk during the transition is non-zero.
UNIT-COST GATINGThe headline C$847/t LOM C1 is gated on completion of Stage 3 in mid-CY29. Stage 1 and Stage 2 deliver volume earlier, but the full unit-cost reduction does not arrive until the new crushing circuit and ore sorting are running.
FXModel fixes CAD/USD at 0.74 across the LOM. NAL reports concentrate sales in USD and incurs costs primarily in CAD; sustained CAD strength compresses margin.
CORPORATEElevra is the post-merger entity of Sayona and Piedmont. Integration overhang, board / management transition risk, and any legacy offtake or marketing arrangements should be inspected.
